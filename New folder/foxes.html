The bat-eared fox is another Kenyan animal that belongs to the dog family. It's large ears are it's defining characteristic. it also feeds on insects lie termites, lives alone or in small groups, and has six more teeth than other foxes.

The bat-eared fox can be found in savannah, arid scrublands, and grassy plains. It lives in countries including Kneya, Tanzania, South Africa, Angola, and Zimbabwe. The IUCN lists them as least concern and as having a stable population, thoughthey are threatened by hunters, disease, and drought.
